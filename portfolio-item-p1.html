CI - Movie Ticketing Interface

Porform the contextual inquiry on how users buy movie tickets.

Quick Details

Participants: 2
Preparation: Task, Initial questions
Deleverables: Recordings, Notes, Documentation

Brief description of the interface

AMC official Theatre website is the movie ticketing website that provides several services for users. Users can browse the movie information, purchase the movie tickets, and order food and drinks in advance. Users can also browse the special events and promotions on their webpages. By registering the membership, users can enjoy the premium services such as reserving seats and large screen viewing.

Preliminary Interview Questions

1. Have you bought movie tickets on AMC websites before?
2. Have you bought movie tickets on any other websites before?
3. How often have you bought it before?
4. When would you find yourself buying any movie tickets online?
5. Who makes the decision related to buying movie tickets online?
6. Would anything influence in the decision making?
7. Does anything particular influence you while choosing movie ticketing website?

Brief description of the user 1

The user 1 is a female user of Chinese origin aged about 26 years. She is now working as a Data Coordinator at the Northwestern University. Typically, she uses computers for her work. Her main mode of transport is subway and bus. She seldom calls Uber unless the circumstances forced her to do so. She usually cooks at home on weekdays. She has a laptop and a tablet, usually using them for browsing and watching Netflix. On weekends, she likes to explore the city by visiting restaurants and museums. She has no experience in buying movie tickets through AMC official website.

Brief description of the user 2

The user 2 is a male user of Taiwanese origin aged about 23 years. He is now a 1st year graduate student studying in Computer Science at Illinois Institute of Technology. He lives in Lakeview area, and his main mode of transport is subway and bus. He goes to school twice a week. Typically, he does his homework and studies with his computer at the café near his home. He has bought movie tickets online on the AMC website once.

Conclusion

The performance of the user 1:
Even though the user 1 don’t have an experience in using the AMC website to purchase tickets, she did know how to complete her task. The user 1 tended to spend more time reviewing the page contexts before she started to do any action such as clicking the button. She didn’t face any problem while she was performing the task.

The performance of the user 2:
When the user 2 clicked See A Movie on the menu bar, he expected the button will prompt him to the new page, but he ended up opening the submenu. He didn’t face any problem while he was performing the task.
